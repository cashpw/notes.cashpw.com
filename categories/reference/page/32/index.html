Leading indicator

Leading indicators are indicators that usually, but not always, change before the [a thing] as a whole changes. They are therefore useful as short-term predictors of the [a thing]. (“Economic Indicator” 2021) Examples Amount of sleep someone gets as a leading indicator for their focus during the day. Bibliography References “Economic Indicator.” 2021. Lagging indicator

Lagging indicators are indicators that usually change after [a thing] as a whole does. […] The unemployment rate is a lagging indicator: employment tends to increase two or three quarters after an upturn in [a thing].
