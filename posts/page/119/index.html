Cognitive dissonance

Cognitive dissonance is typically experienced as psychological stress when persons participate in an action that goes against one or more of those things. Moving the goalposts

Moving the goalposts (or shifting the goalposts) is a metaphor, derived from goal-based sports, that means to change the rule or criterion (goal) of a process or competition while it is still in progress, in such a way that the new goal offers one side an advantage or disadvantage. Cargo cult

These cults were first described in Melanesia in the wake of contact with allied military forces during the Second World War. The basic idea is this: Smear a food-grade Drying oil onto a cast iron pan, and then bake it above the oil’s smoke point. This will initiate the release of free radicals and polymerization. The more drying the oil, the harder the polymer. So start with the right oil.
