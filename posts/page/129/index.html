Speak directly to the audience

Consider a public argument between two individuals. There are three participants: the two individuals and the audience around them – the public. It can be easier to win the argument, for some definitions of win, to speak past the person you’re arguing with and instead talk directly to the audience; ignore them altogether. Moving the goalposts

Moving the goalposts (or shifting the goalposts) is a metaphor, derived from goal-based sports, that means to change the rule or criterion (goal) of a process or competition while it is still in progress, in such a way that the new goal offers one side an advantage or disadvantage.
