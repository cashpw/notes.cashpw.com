Framing

In the social sciences, framing comprises a set of concepts and theoretical perspectives on how individuals, groups, and societies organize, perceive, and communicate about reality. Framing can manifest in thought or interpersonal communication. Frames in thought consist of the mental representations, interpretations, and simplifications of reality [similar: Mental model]. Frames in communication consist of the communication of frames between different actors. Framing is a key component of sociology, the study of social interaction among humans....
