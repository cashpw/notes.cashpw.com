k-vertex-connected graph

In graph theory, a connected graph [Connected graph] \(G\) is said to be k-vertex-connected (or k-connected) if it has more than k vertices and remains connected whenever fewer than k vertices are removed. (“K-Vertex-Connected Graph” 2023) Bibliography “K-Vertex-Connected Graph.” 2023. Wikipedia, April. https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=K-vertex-connected_graph&oldid=1151236919. Biconnected graph

In graph theory [Graph theory], a biconnected graph [Graph] is a connected [Connected graph] and “nonseparable” graph, meaning that if any one vertex were to be removed, the graph will remain connected. Therefore a biconnected graph has no articulation vertices. The property of being 2-connected [k-vertex-connected graph] is equivalent to biconnectivity, except that the complete graph of two vertices is usually not regarded as 2-connected.
