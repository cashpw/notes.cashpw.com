Personal knowledge management

Personal knowledge management (PKM) is a process of collecting information that a person uses to gather, classify, store, search, retrieve and share knowledge in their daily activities and the way in which these processes support work activities. It is a response to the idea that knowledge workers need to be responsible for their own growth and learning. Style Guide

A style guide for my personal knowledge management system (powered by org-roam): Prose I try to write with an emphasis on clear communication. Use plain language Use serial commas Vary the length of sentences Be useful Avoid qualifying language Be explicit Use sentence case in headings and titles Use “Sentence case”, rather than “Title Case” in headings and titles.
