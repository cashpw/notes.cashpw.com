Lime popping

A body firing problem where particles of calcium sulphate in the fired or bisque fired body slowly absorb water from the air, expand, and pop out a section of the body. These particles can be contamination or naturally occurring contaminants in the clay. (“Lime Popping” n.d.) Bibliography References “Lime Popping.” n.d.
