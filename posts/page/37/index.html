Theory of mind

In psychology, theory of mind refers to the capacity to understand other people by ascribing mental states to them (that is, surmising what is happening in their mind). This includes the knowledge that others' mental states may be different from one’s own states and include beliefs, desires, intentions, emotions, and thoughts. Negative partisanship

Negative partisanship is the tendency of some voters to form their political opinions primarily in opposition to political parties they dislike. Whereas traditional partisanship involves supporting the policy positions of one’s own party, its negative counterpart in turn means opposing those positions of a disliked party.
