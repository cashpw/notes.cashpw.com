Directed acyclic graph

In mathematics, particularly graph theory, and computer science, a directed acyclic graph (DAG) is a Directed graph [Directed graph] with no directed cycles. That is, it consists of vertices [Vertices] and edges [Edges] (also called arcs), with each edge directed from one vertex to another, such that following those directions will never form a closed loop. A directed graph is a DAG if and only if it can be topologically ordered [Topological order], by arranging the vertices as a linear ordering that is consistent with all edge directions....
